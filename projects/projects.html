Math Undergraduate Research

Lie Symmetries and Differential Equations

Research into the theoretical foundations of symmetry methods for differential equations, including Lie Symmetry Groups of ODEs, PDEs, and systems, infinitesimal generators and their prolongations, and discrete symmetries. Also investigated more advanced symmetry methods, such as variational symmetries, general (non-geometric) symmetries, and conservation laws. Currently investigating the application of these methods to various physical problems arising from the physical sciences.

Research Mentor: Dr. Tamas Wiandt

Primality Testing and Factorization Methods

A survey paper of the various methods used in testing the primality of large numbers and obtaining the prime factorization of large composites. Partnered with Jodie Miu, who unfortunately could not complete her portion of the paper - I am the sole author of the Primality testing sections, as well as other parts of the paper listed under the Author information at the end.

There are also a limited number of C implementations of the early primality methods - I plan to add implementations of more sophisticated methods, as well as possibly complete the factorization portion myself in the future.

Assigned By: Dr. Anurag Agarwal

MIPS

Wrote a 3-in-a-row puzzle solver in MIPS assembly language, using a simple stepback algorithm, and laid out a simple multicycle MIPS CPU in the logisim circuitry program. Geometric Algebra

Laid out in circuitry the bare bones implementation of a hardware supported geometric product, based on an assembly implementation also included, and presented it to the other participants in a Seminar on Geometric Algebra, to suggest a way to make Geometric Algebra more competitive with traditional Linear Algebra methods in computing. I plan to add an introductory paper on Geometric Algebra, and perhaps a GA math library, to this project in the future.
A download link for the logisim circuit simulator can be found here.
